About

My practice exists in the liminal space between laboratory and studio. Trained in molecular neuroscience and drawn to the visual complexity of biological systems, I develop works that make the hidden logic of life legible. From neural organoid cultures to generative algorithms modeled on dendritic growth, each project is an act of translation—rendering the invisible visible.

I believe that art and science are not parallel disciplines but entangled ones. The same patterns that govern axonal pathfinding appear in urban networks, in fungal mycelia, in the flow of information through social systems. My work maps these isomorphisms, creating experiences that resonate on both aesthetic and analytical registers.
